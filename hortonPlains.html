Horton Plains

overview

Horton Plains Is known as Sri Lanka's only high-altitude national park. It lies between the country's 2nd and third highest mountains, kirigallpottha and Totupolakanda which lie to the west and northeast of the plain respectively. This area lies in a plain located about 2100m above sea level comprises of rolling grasslands set within tropical mountains and cloud forests. Horton Plains is rich in biodiversity and endemic species of Sri Lanka can be found mostly in this region. The mean annual rainfall gathers more than 2,000 millimetres and the average temperature is about 13 degrees of Celcius. Horton Plans is also a very popular tourist destination in Sri Lanka situated 8 kilometres from Ohiya and 32 kilometers from Nuwara Eliya.

5 Places to Explore

WORLD'S END

The Horton planes plateau comes to a sudden end at the world's end. It is a sheer cliff with a drop of about 4000 feet. It is one of the most visited places in the park

BAKER'S FALLS

Baker’s falls is one of the popular waterfalls situated in the Horton plains national park. It is 20 meters high and situated on the Belihul Oya. It flows over a wide rock into a gorge filed with rhododendrons.

BAMBARAKANDA FALLS

Bambarakanda Falls is known as the tallest waterfall in Sri Lanka. This place is one of the most spectacular places during the monsoon season. Bambarakanda Falls plummets down from a Hight of 263 meters.The waterfall is created by the Kuda Oya which is a tributary of the Walawe River.

GOMMOLLI KANDA MOUNTAIN

Gommolli Kanda Mountain is located in the Horton plains national park. It stands at a height of 2,034 meters which continues to be the 13th highest mountain in Sri Lanka. This mountain is located at about a halfway up the Belihuloya village in the Sabaragamuwa Province. This place offers some of the This place offers some of the best spectacular views in Sri Lanka which one can enjoy in a clam breeze and silence environment.

BELIHULOYA

Bellihuloya is famous for its waterfalls such as Bamabarakanda falls, Hirrikatuwa Oya Pahanthuda Ella, Surathalee Ella and Papulagala Ella. There is a village located 150km away from Colombo called the village of Belihuloya. The Samanala dam is located 9km from the village and is the 2nd largest dam in Sri Lanka. Belihuloya is famous as a transition point between the wet and dry zones of Sri Lanka.
